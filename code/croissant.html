Croissant

𐙚

Home / Croissant

Ingredients

  • 140 ml water
  • 140 ml milk
  • 55 grams sugar
  • 40 grams unsalted butter
  • 10 grams salt
  • 10 grams instant yeast
  • 250 grams cold unsalted butter
  • 1 egg, for egg wash

Preparation

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, salt, and instant yeast.
  • Add the softened butter, water, and milk to the dry ingredients.
  • Mix until a dough forms, then knead for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  • Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour.

Instruction

  1. 1. Laminate the Dough:
    • On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough into a rectangle about 1/2 inch thick.
    • Place the 250 grams of cold butter between two pieces of parchment paper and pound it with a rolling pin into a rectangle about 1/4 inch thick.
    • Remove the top parchment paper and place the butter sheet on the lower third of the dough rectangle.
    • Fold the dough over the butter so it is completely encased. Seal the edges.
    • Roll the dough into a long rectangle, then fold it into thirds (like a letter).
    • Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and chill for 1 hour. Repeat the rolling and folding process two more times, chilling for at least 1 hour between each turn.
  2. 2. Shape the Croissants:
    • Roll the dough out to a rectangle about 1/4 inch thick.
    • Cut the dough into long triangles with a base of about 5 inches.
    • Roll each triangle tightly from the base to the tip to form a croissant shape, ensuring the tip is tucked under the bottom of the croissant to prevent it from unrolling during baking.
    • Place the shaped croissants on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving space between each for expansion.
    • Cover loosely with plastic wrap and let them rise in a warm place until nearly doubled in size, about 1 to 2 hours.
  3. 3. Bake the Croissants:
    • Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
    • Beat the egg with a tablespoon of water and brush it over the croissants to give them a shiny, golden finish.
    • Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes or until they are golden brown and puffed up.
    • Let the croissants cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Finishing

    Here some toppings you can add to enhance their flavor and appearance:
    • Seeds and Nuts
    • Cheese
    • Herbs and Spices
    • Coarse Sugar
    • Chocolate, Strawberry or Matcha
    • Fruit
    Eat your freshly baked croissants warm, ideally the same day they are baked for the best texture and flavor. Cofee or tea is recommended for a fantastic combination.
